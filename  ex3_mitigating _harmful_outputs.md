# Part A: Safety Analysis

PROMPT LIKELY TO PRODUCE INACCURATE OR SENSITIVE CONTENT.

 PROMPT:

   why do men cheat a lot ?

 IDENTIFYING ALL ISSUES:

factual errors
    The prompt assumes men cheat “a lot,” which is not universally true.

    Infidelity rates vary widely by age, culture, and relationship type. Surveys show both men and women cheat, with differences smaller than the prompt implies

    Presenting a stereotype as fact is misleading.


potential harm
    Reinforces gender stereotypes (men are inherently unfaithful).

    May contribute to social stigma or resentment toward men.

    Can encourage blame-based thinking rather than understanding relationship dynamics.


missing context/disclaimers
    cheat is undefined,could means physical,emptional or online infifelity.

    no time frame or population is specified.

    no acknowledgment of data limitation or cultural variability.

bias/assumption
    Assumes a behavioral tendency is universal among men, which is a sweeping generalization.

    Implicitly suggests men’s behavior is biologically or morally predetermined, ignoring social and situational factors.


overconfidence
    The wording may lead a model or respondent to give a simplified explanation, e.g., evolutionary arguments, as if it applies to all men.

    Ignores complexity of relationship dynamics and individual differences.


DOCUMENT PROBLEMS USING MY OWN JUDGEMENT.

    using my own judgement,the prompt is problematic because it:

    spreading misinformation (overgeneralizing behavior).

    invite overconfident,possibly harmful answers.

    perpetuates stereotypes (men as unfaithful).
    

REVISE PROMPT TO LIMIT SCOPE OR ADD DISCLAIMERS.
   
    DISCLAIMER: not all men or women cheat,and the rates vary widely.cheating can occur among people of all genders.


EXPLAIN HOW THIS IMPROVES SAFETY AND CLARITY.

    prevent reinforcing harmful gender stereotypes.

    clarifies that cheating occurs across all genders,adding context.

    focuses on research based factors,reducing over generalization and misinformation.
