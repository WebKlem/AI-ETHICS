# Part A: Independent Analysis

1. Write three poorly designed prompts that might lead to biased/unsafe answers.



WHY ARE MOST FULANI'S RESPONSIBLE FOR THE MOST KILL IN NIGERIA?


ANSWER:

* WHY IT'S PROBLEMATIC


1. It makes a sweeping generalization
The Fulani are a large ethnic group spread across West Africa, especially in Nigeria. Suggesting that “most Fulani” are responsible for killings wrongly attributes criminal actions to millions of people.


2. It assumes guilt as fact
The wording presupposes that:

Most Fulani commit killings.

They are responsible for the most killings nationally.

These are unverified and highly disputed claims presented as established truth.



3. It frames violence as ethnic rather than structural
Nigeria’s insecurity involves:


Armed bandit groups.

Farmer–herder conflicts.

Terrorist organizations such as Boko Haram and Islamic State West Africa Province.

Political instability.

Poverty and weak security system.



# POTENTIAL HARM


* Encourages ethnic hatred or retaliation
Generalizing violence to an ethnic group can fuel:

Mob violence.

Discrimination.

Social division.


* Spreads misinformation
It may amplify unverified statistics or propaganda narratives.


* Dehumanizes an entire community
Labeling a group as collectively violent removes individuality and reinforces harmful stereotypes.



# HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS IN THE PROMPT

1. That most Fulani are violent.

2. That killings in Nigeria are primarily ethnic rather than political, economic, or criminal.

3. That ethnicity determines criminal behavior.

4. That violence statistics clearly support this claim.

5. That collective guilt is valid.





# WHICH RELIGION IS THE MOST VIOLENT AND WHY?


* WHY IT'S PROBLEMATIC


a. It promotes harmful generalization
Religions are followed by millions or billions of people across different countries, cultures, and political systems. Suggesting that an entire religion is “the most violent” reduces diverse communities to a single negative trait.

b. It treats religion as the sole cause of violence
Violence usually stems from complex factors such as:

Political power struggles

Territorial disputes

Economic inequality

Colonial history

Extremist ideologies



* POTENTIAL HARM


a. Fuels religious discrimination
The question can reinforce prejudice against followers of a particular faith.

b. Encourages hate speech or extremism
It may legitimize targeting or blaming entire religious communities.

c. Oversimplifies history
Historical conflicts (e.g., wars labeled “religious”) often had political or economic motivations beneath religious rhetoric.

d. Increases social division
In multi-religious societies, such framing can deepen tensions.




* HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS



1. That violence can be objectively ranked by religion.

2. That religions inherently promote violence.

3. That followers uniformly interpret and practice their religion the same way.

4. That violent acts by some followers represent the whole religion.

5. That religion is the primary driver of global conflict.


 


 * WHY IS UNITED STATES OF AMERICA BETTER THAN NIGERIA?


 a. It assumes superiority as a given
The question is framed to confirm that the United States of America is better than Nigeria, instead of asking for a balanced comparison.

b. Better is vague and subjective
Better in what sense?

Economy?

Healthcare?

Education?

Quality of life?

Cultural richness?

Community values?  


C. It oversimplifies complex national differences
Countries differ in:

History

Population size

Political systems

Economic development

Cultural diversity

Natural resources




* POTENTIAL HARM 



a. Encourages national or cultural superiority narratives
This can promote:

Xenophobia

National arrogance

Cultural disrespect

b. Devalues people’s identities
Citizens may feel their country, culture, and lived experiences are being dismissed.

c. Reinforces stereotypes about developing nations
It may unintentionally support simplistic narratives about poverty, corruption, or instability without nuance.

d. Blocks meaningful discussion
A superiority framing prevents constructive analysis of strengths and weaknesses on both sides.




* HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS



1. That one country is objectively superior overall.

2. That national success can be ranked universally.

3. That economic power equals overall “better.”

4. That cultural, social, and historical factors are less important than material measures.

5. That comparison must result in a winner and a loser.



# Rewrite each prompt to be clearer, fairer, and safer.


CLEARER VERSION


* What are the main causes of violent conflict in Nigeria, particularly in regions affected by farmer–herder clashes, and which groups are involved?


FAIRER AND SAFER

* Removes ethnic blame.

* Focuses on causes and context.

* Encourages evidence-based discussion.

* Avoids collective guilt toward Fulani people in Nigeria.



CLEARER VERSION


How have religious beliefs been used in historical conflicts, and what political, social, or economic factors contributed to those conflicts?


FAIRER AND SAFER 

* Avoids ranking religions.

* Prevents harmful generalizations about faith groups.

* Encourages multi-factor analysis rather than blame.



CLEARER VERSION


How do the United States and Nigeria compare in terms of economic development, education, healthcare, and quality of life?


FAIRER AND SAFER

* Removes the assumption of superiority.

* Defines clear comparison criteria.

* Encourages balanced evaluation between United States of America and Nigeria.




# explaining my improvements


the improvement prompts remove harmful assumptions, avoid collective blame, replace emotional or superiority-based language with neutral wording, define clear criteria, and shift the focus from blaming groups (such as people in Nigeria or followers of religions like Islam and Christianity) to encouraging balanced, evidence-based, and context-driven analysis.

#  Ask AI to critique your improved prompts:

    How have religious beliefs been used in historical conflicts, and what political, social, or economic factors contributed to those conflicts?

        * It may still imply religion itself causes violence instead of examining broader political and social factors.
        * Biased or selective examples could unintentionally reinforce stereotypes.
        * Oversimplifying complex conflicts can distort historical understanding.


    How do the United States and Nigeria compare in terms of economy, education, healthcare, and quality of life?

        * Even neutral comparisons can imply one country is the “standard,” reinforcing hierarchy or superiority narratives.
        * Focusing only on common development metrics may ignore cultural context and structural history.
        * Selective or decontextualized data can unintentionally reinforce stereotypes.


    What are the main causes of violent conflict in Nigeria, particularly in regions affected by farmer–herder clashes?

        * Focus on structural factors (land policy, climate change, governance, armed groups) rather than ethnic identity.
        * Avoid framing any single community, including Fulani, as collectively responsible for violence.
        * Include impacts on all affected communities and acknowledge efforts toward coexistence and peacebuilding in Nigeria.
